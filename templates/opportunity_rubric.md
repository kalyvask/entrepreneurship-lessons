## Must-haves (binary — opportunity must satisfy ALL of these)

- [ ] **Inflection.** Clear, defensible change vector. Why now?
- [ ] **Non-consensus.** Sophisticated people in the space would disagree with the insight.
- [ ] **Narrow targetable wedge.** I could manually identify 50 specific customers today.
- [ ] **Desperation signal.** At least 3 of the 4 desperation markers visible in interviews.
- [ ] **Unit economics plausible.** LTV/CAC > 3 plausible at scale; payback < 24 months.
- [ ] **Personal fit.** I can credibly stay with this for 10 years.
- [ ] **No deal-breakers.** No items on my deal-breaker list (below).

---

**Discipline:** Do not bend the rubric to fit a favorite candidate. If a favorite candidate fails a must-have, the right move is either to: (a) update the must-have explicitly with a note about why, or (b) accept that the candidate isn't the right one. Unconscious rubric drift to support a favorite is one of the most common failure modes.
