# Positioning Statement — Template

Written at [Stage 04](../stages/04_value_hypothesis.md), after you've chosen a **market type** (see [`../frameworks/market_type.md`](../frameworks/market_type.md)) and drafted a value hypothesis. The artifact that says, in one sentence a stranger could repeat: *who this is for, what it is, and why it beats the alternative they have today.*

This is the external, market-facing one-liner. It is the sibling of the internal [`value_hypothesis.md`](value_hypothesis.md) (what / who / how): the value hypothesis is for **you to test**; the positioning statement is for **the customer to recognize themselves in**.

## Step 1 — Name your market type

Positioning is determined by market type. Pick one (see [`../frameworks/market_type.md`](../frameworks/market_type.md)):

- [ ] **Existing market** — customers already buy a solution; you're better on the known axis
- [ ] **Resegment (niche)** — an underserved slice of an existing market
- [ ] **Resegment (low-cost)** — good-enough for materially less
- [ ] **New market** — customers don't exist yet; you enable something newly possible
- [ ] **Clone** — a proven model localized to a new geography

## Slot-by-slot guidance

| Slot | Discipline |
|---|---|
| **target customer** | The narrow *who* from your value hypothesis — specific enough to identify 50 of them, not "mid-market companies." |
| **need / problem** | The one hot, recurring pain (not a list). Use the customer's own words from interviews. |
| **category** | The mental box the customer files you in. **Determined by market type** — see Step 3. |
| **key benefit** | One benefit, the one that matters most to *this* customer. Not three. |
| **primary alternative** | What they actually use today — often a spreadsheet, a workaround, or *doing nothing*, not a named competitor. |
| **differentiator** | The structural reason you win that an incumbent can't easily copy. If it's "we're better/cheaper," ask *why structurally.* |

---

## Step 3 — Match the category and alternative to your market type

- **Existing market** → category is known; alternative is the **incumbent**; differentiate on **performance**.
  *"…is a [known category] that [does it faster/better]. Unlike [incumbent], we [measurable edge]."*
- **Resegment** → category is known; alternative is the **generic incumbent**; differentiate on **fit to the segment**.
  *"…is the [category] **for** [specific segment]. Unlike [generic incumbent], we [segment-specific value]."*
- **New market** → there is **no clean category or competitor**; the alternative is **the status quo / doing nothing**; differentiate on a **newly-possible capability**.
  *"…is a new way to [job], now possible because [inflection]. Unlike [the manual status quo], we [new capability]."*

---

## Step 4 — Test it

- [ ] **Self-identification.** Would the target customer read this and think *"that's me"* — without a sales rep explaining it? If they'd need convincing, narrow the *who*.
- [ ] **Real alternative.** Is the "primary alternative" what they *actually* do today (including nothing), not a strawman?
- [ ] **The "anybody could say that" test.** Could a competitor put their name in your statement and have it read just as true? If yes, the differentiator is generic — sharpen it.
- [ ] **Why-me / why-now.** Can you defend, in one line, why *you* and why *now*? (Ties back to the insight — see [`../stages/01_insight_and_idea.md`](../stages/01_insight_and_idea.md).)
- [ ] **Order-of-magnitude.** For the benefit that matters, are you ~10× better than the alternative, or merely incrementally so? Incremental rarely moves a customer off the status quo.

## Common failure modes

| Failure | Fix |
|---|---|
| Positioning to "everyone" | Use the narrow *who* from the value hypothesis |
| Claiming a new category when you're a resegment | Use the known category; you avoid education costs (see [`../frameworks/market_type.md`](../frameworks/market_type.md)) |
| Differentiator anyone could claim | Name the *structural* edge, not "better/faster/cheaper" |
| Alternative is a strawman | Name what they truly do today, including doing nothing |
| Feature list instead of one benefit | Pick the single benefit this *who* cares about most |
