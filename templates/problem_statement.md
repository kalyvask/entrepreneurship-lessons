## What's NOT a problem (but you might be tempted to think is)

The risky boundary. Things that LOOK like problems but aren't:

- Customers say it'd be nice; nobody is desperate
- Customers describe the problem but never spent money / time on it
- Customers say "I'd love that" but their behavior is identical to people who don't have the problem
- The problem is real but the segment is wrong (you've talked to people who don't actually have authority)

If any of the above describe what you've heard: the problem statement isn't ready. Go back to more discovery.
