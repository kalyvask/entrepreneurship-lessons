## Opening (2 min)

**Don't pitch.** Conversational tone.

> *"Thanks for taking the time. I'm researching [SPECIFIC TOPIC] in [SPECIFIC SEGMENT]. Trying to understand the real workflow — not from outside, but from people actually doing it. I'm not selling anything. Just want to learn from you."*

> *"Mind if I record? It helps me listen better and capture exact language rather than paraphrase. Recording stays with me; happy to send you a copy."*

---

## Their context (5 min)

Easy warm-up. Confirm context.

- *"Tell me about your role."*
- *"What does a typical week look like?"*
- *"How are you measured?"*
- *"How long have you been doing this?"*

---

## The terrain (15 min)

The heart of the interview. Open questions. Follow energy. Probe specifics.

### Their reality

- *"What's been the most frustrating thing recently in [topic area]?"*
- *"Walk me through the last time you tried to [job related to topic]."*
- *"What do you currently do when [trigger event]?"*

### Workarounds (highest-signal question)

- *"How do you handle that today?"*
- *"Has anyone in your organization tried to solve this?"*
- *"What tools / spreadsheets / hacks have you built?"*

### Pain magnitude

- *"What did it cost you the last time?"*
- *"How often does this come up?"*
- *"Is this your top priority, or is something else above it?"*

### Magic wand

- *"If you had unlimited resources, what would you change about [process]?"*
- *"What's something that should be possible in your work but isn't?"*

### Change

- *"What's different in [space] now compared to 2 years ago?"*
- *"What's exciting? What's concerning?"*

### Best practices

- *"Who tackles this well? Why?"*
- *"Are there companies / industries / countries you look to for ideas?"*

## Close (3 min)

- *"Before we wrap — who else should I be talking to in this space?"*
- *"Specifically, [adjacent role, or different segment]?"*
- *"Mind making an intro? Happy to draft a short email you can forward."*
- *"Is there anything I should have asked that I didn't?"*
- *"Anything I can do that would be useful to you?"*
- *"If a question surfaces later, OK if I follow up?"*

Thank sincerely. End on time.

---

## Live notes — separate file

Capture in a separate file (or column) during the call:
- Direct quotes (in quotation marks)
- Specific facts (numbers, dates, names)
- Workarounds they mentioned
- Stories they told (with detail)
- Emotion (frustration, excitement, anxiety) — when and about what

**Do not edit during the call.** Write fast. Edit in the debrief.

## Reminders

- **90/10 rule.** Listen 90%. Talk 10%.
- **Open-ended questions.** Start with what / why / how.
- **Past behavior > stated future intent.**
- **Don't fill silences.**
- **Don't pitch.**
- **Don't seek validation.**
- **The interviewee is the expert.** You are there to learn.
